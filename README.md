Overview & Specs

The M224A1 is not a vanilla mortar. It is carried assemble by one person in the launcher slot, is loaded by the gunner without leaving the gun, and does not have a gunner's sight. 

- Carried in launcher slot, placed through keybind with a "ghost". 
- Deployed M224 acts as a turret. Reload is via the player inventory. 
- Custom Animations & Particle Effects 
- Reposition the tube or pick it up through look-interactions.

User Manual

The M224A1 Tube itself is classified as a ROCKET LAUNCHER and occupies that slot. 

Note: The inventory item itself is NOT the fireable variant. Placing it on the ground will do nothing.

The Shells are classified as GRENADES and can be found under that slot.


Placement:

When occupying a position to fire from, equip the tube from the launcher slot. Once in hands, placement mode can be entered through hitting the default action key ("F" on PC). This can be rebound through the settings window.

After that, you should see a moving M224 "Ghost" that tracks your camera position. Orient yourself on flat ground towards your direction of fire and hit the default fire button ("LCLICK" on PC).

Repositioning Note: You must be within <GET DATA> m of the reposition point. The ghost will disapear if you leave that radius.

At this point, the Mortar Turret is emplaced and ready to enter. It can be picked up & repositioned via default interactions while looking at the tube. 

UI & Firing:

You have entered the Mortar and are ready to fire. By default, the weapon comes UNLOADED. This is by design, as once a round is loaded it can only be fired. 

The default selection is:
High Explosive | CHARGE 0 | DIRECT IMPACT

These selections can be changed by looking down at the trigger guard and interacting with the options there. 

NOTE: Changing ammunition type applies to the NEXT loaded round. If you need to fire WP, you must either fire your loaded round or select the option prior to loading. 

UI:

On the top of your screen is a scrolling azimuth display. It displays both mils and degrees. 

Slightly off center in the bottom right is your elevation bubble. Please reference the graphic below. 



NOTE: The UI will glow at night/under night vision to assist with visibility.

Firing:

Once dialed in on the proper bearing and elevation, it is the default fire weapon interaction. Reloading is done via the default reload action, the round is loaded once the sound ends. 

NOTE: The muzzle has an overpressure damage radius of about 1 meter. Keep nearby heads down.
